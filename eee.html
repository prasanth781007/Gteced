About EEE Department

The Department of Electrical & Electronics Engineering at GTEC was established to meet the growing demand for skilled professionals in the field of electrical systems and electronics. Our department offers a comprehensive 4-year B.E. program with a focus on both theoretical foundations and practical applications.

Our department is committed to providing quality education and fostering research in electrical engineering, power systems, electronics, and control systems. We have well-equipped laboratories, experienced faculty members, and strong industry connections that provide our students with excellent learning opportunities.

The department focuses on emerging areas like Power Electronics, Renewable Energy Systems, Control Systems, Electric Vehicles, and Smart Grid Technologies to prepare students for the rapidly evolving technological landscape.

Laboratories & Facilities

Power Electronics Laboratory

Comprehensive facility for studying power electronic devices, converters, and their applications in modern power systems.

Key Equipment:
  • Power Electronic Converters
  • Thyristor/MOSFET/IGBT Modules
  • Digital Storage Oscilloscopes
  • Power Supplies and Load Banks

Electric Circuits Laboratory

Hands-on facility for designing, building, and testing various electrical circuits including AC/DC circuits and network theorems.

Key Equipment:
  • Digital Multimeters
  • Function Generators
  • Power Supplies
  • Breadboards & Components

Electrical Machine Laboratory

Facility for studying various electrical machines including transformers, DC machines, induction motors, and synchronous machines.

Key Equipment:
  • DC Motors & Generators
  • AC Induction Motors
  • Synchronous Machines
  • Loading Arrangements

Control System Laboratory

State-of-the-art facility for studying control system theory, feedback systems, and automation applications.

Key Equipment:
  • PID Controllers
  • PLC Training Systems
  • MATLAB with Control Toolbox
  • Servo Motor Systems

Power System Simulation Laboratory

Equipped with advanced simulation software for power system analysis, load flow studies, and fault analysis.

Key Equipment:
  • ETAP Software
  • MATLAB/Simulink
  • PowerWorld Simulator
  • PSPICE

Measurement and Instrumentation Laboratory

Specialized facility for experiments in electrical measurements, transducers, and instrumentation systems.

Key Equipment:
  • Digital Storage Oscilloscopes
  • Transducer Training Kits
  • Bridges for Measurement
  • Data Acquisition Systems
